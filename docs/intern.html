Social Media Marketing Intern

EFI Connection

Week 1-5

I began my internship on December 18th, 2023. In the the first five weeks I've been here, I have become acquainted with all the people and processes that happen here. EFI Connection is a wire harness shop that specializes in GM TP1, LT1, and LS-Series V8 engines. Other branches include EFI Conversion, BenchForce, and LS-Wiring. My job is to monitor the socials of these businesses/sites and create content for them. I am also taking videos and photos for the EFI Connection products. Ultimately, during these weeks, I have been taking footage, scheduling posts, and studying the parts we sell.

After this first inital post, I will provide weekly updates to this blog page.

Week 6

During week six, I have been organizing blog posts to schedule on two of the company sites. These blog posts talk about current industry products and car information. We had a meeting about Outreach Projects on Friday to figure out how we should increase our apperance amongst car enthusiaists. I also brainstormed ideas for how to increase reach through our BenchForce and EFI Connection site. Futhermore, I am looking into a content scheduler for all of our socials, so posting content and social analytics can be easier to access.

Week 7

Week seven was filled with little projects here and there. I fixed some of the Bitmojis I was tasked with making and finished out blog scheduling for Bench Force and LS Wiring. I got the confirmation from my supervisor to use Loomly as a content scheduler, so I set up an account for that. Customers had sent in photos of their products, so I used their photos and reviews to make social media posts. So far, I completed one for Bench Force and one for EFI Connection, and scheduled them through Loomly to test out the platform. In addition, to add multiple links to one site, I created a Linktree with all of our websites and social media accounts. This week ended with brainstorming ideas for a Valentine's Day post.

Week 8

This week I scheduled more review posts for the EFI Connection Instagram and Bench Force Facebook. I worked with one of my supervisors to officialize where connector photos were going to be stored. I captured some content of production for the EFI Connection Facebook and Instagram. This also included capturing content for a Valentine's Day post. The videos will be worked on this weekend so they can be posted.

Week 9

Week nine I edited the Valentine's Day Posts for EFI Connection, Bench Force, and LS Wiring. I scheduled and posted them for Valentine's Day, and they received very good feedback. I met with one of my supervisors, Chris, to talk about how we are going to store photos and the type of size we are looking for. We also worked on figuring out the settings for capturing macrophotography photos. I worked with the owner, Mike, to take some footage of CNC machining and wire harness production. Our hope is to produce a "How it's Made" type of video. Finally, I worked on setting up a campaign strategy so EFI Connection had a strategic framework for Social Media Marketing.

Week 10

During week ten, a lot of content was shot. We are working on creating a video for one of our partners to look at and get a sense of what the work/shop is like. In addition, more content was shot of the CNC machine for a production video. I began editing the video this week, I am hoping to get it completed by next week. I also scheduled some posts and updated the Linktree on our socials. Chris came in and worked on our camera set up for macro photos as well.

Week 11

This week, I was unable to put many hours in at EFI Connection. In preparation for the Alternative Spring Break trip I leave for on Friday, I have had copious amounts of work to do. During the few hours I was there though, I created and scheduled posts for the week I am gone. This included an Employee Appreciation Day post and a CNC Machine work video. I also worked on the "How it's Made" video. Slow progress as I am a perfectionist, and I want the video to turn out well.

Week 12

During Week 12, I took some footage and pictures of the shop. Some of the footage is going into a video project for EFI and the pictures were used for social content. We had a meeting on Monday to discuss storage within the NAS system. I continued working on video editing and content scheduling.

Week 13

Week 13 I worked on making progress with the LT1 video. In addition, I practiced taking pictures of wire pigtails for the EFI Connection shop on Ebay. Some time was spent color correcting the images since it was looking different than the actual product.

Week 14

I made more progress on the LT1 video this week. Unfortunately, I was unable to put in many hours as circumstances came up.

Week 15

After a discussion with my supervisor, he made some suggestions on what I should fix up in the video. He also metioned that he would like an intro and outro for the video. I worked on making those fixes and creating a draft of an intro.

Week 16

I was not able to work this week as we were in Daytona Beach, Florida for NCA Nationals.

Week 17 and 18 + Final Review

Since the semester is coming to a close, I informed my supervisor that my course work would need my attention throughout these weeks. Because of this, I could not put in hours for week 17 and 18. However, I will be continuing my internship over the summer.

During my internship at EFI Connection LLC, I managed social media accounts, captured shop activities through photography and videography, and produced and edited videos. I curated engaging content for our online presence, showcasing the shop's craftsmanship and expertise. My role contributed to enhancing brand visibility and fostering connections with our audience.

The concepts and skills acquired through DIGIT coursework greatly intersected with my internship responsibilities:

  • Website Development: Creating websites for DIGIT projects honed my skills in web design and development, which indirectly influenced my approach to social media management. Understanding user experience (UX) principles helped optimize content presentation on various platforms, maximizing viewer engagement and interaction.
  • Data Visualization: The proficiency gained in crafting data visualizations proved supplemental in analyzing social media performance metrics. Utilizing Excel for data visualization allowed me to identify patterns, trends, and areas for improvement, informing strategic decisions for content creation and distribution.
  • Filming and Editing: Proficiency of Adobe Premiere Pro and Lightroom, obtained in DIGIT coursework, empowered me to produce captivating video content for our target audiences.

Key Learnings:

  • Adaptability and Versatility: Embracing diverse responsibilities fostered adaptability and versatility in navigating dynamic work environments. Whether crafting social media posts, conducting video shoots, or analyzing data, the ability to pivot between tasks was important to meeting project objectives.
  • Continuous Learning: The internship served as a catalyst for continuous learning and professional growth. Each challenge presented an opportunity to expand skill sets, refine techniques, and explore innovative solutions.
  • Industry Knowledge: Gaining insight in engine wire harness production widened my knowledge about the industry and its' customers. Defining target audiences and obtaining relationships with customers was essential in propelling company initiatives.

In conclusion, the internship experience has been immensely rewarding as I got to apply theoretical knowledge to a real-world industry in the realm of digital media management. As I continue on in this role, I carry forward insights, skills, and memories that will undoubtedly shape my future endeavors in the digital landscape.

Thank you to the internship team, mentors, and colleagues for their guidance, support, and collaboration throughout this enriching journey.
